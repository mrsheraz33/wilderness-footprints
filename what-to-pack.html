WHAT TO PACK

Essential Gear & Equipment for Kilimanjaro

It is difficult to give trekkers an all encompassing list of clothing and equipment required, however, we think we have pretty much covered it below. The days on Kilimanjaro will usually warm up where you may be able to wear just a shirt. Other days you will need considerably more warm clothing. As the altitude increases, the nights become cold to very cold. A good quality sleeping bag and warm clothing are essential.

No Showers on Kilimanjaro: There are no showers or washing facilities on Kilimanjaro. You only have what you pack until your return to Arusha.

Clothing

Waterproof Jacket – breathable with hood Essential
Waterproof pants – breathable Essential
Insulated Jacket, synthetic or down Essential
Jacket – soft shell or fleece
Light weight short sleeve shirt (moisture-wicking)
2 Long Sleeve Shirts (moisture-wicking)
2 Pairs of hiking pants
1 Pair fleece Pants
1 Pair long underwear (top & bottom) Recommended
3 Pairs underwear briefs (moisture-wicking)
1 Pair skins Recommended
2 Sport Bra – women Recommended

Headwear

Wide brimmed hat for sun protection Recommended
Scarf or neck gaiter Recommended
Knit wool hat (beanie) Essential
Balaclava Optional

Hand Wear

Down mittens or gloves (waterproof recommended)
Light weight waterproof gloves Optional
Glove Liners Optional
Hand warmers Optional

Footwear

Hiking boots (Goretex, well worn in) Essential
Running shoes or sandals for camp Optional
3 Pair thick wool/synthetic socks
3 Pair sock liners Optional
Gaiters (ankle length, Goretex) Highly Recommended
Knee supports Highly Recommended

Equipment

Sleeping bag (-15°C rated) Essential
Sleeping bag liner Optional
Trekking poles (extendable) Highly Recommended
Head lamp with extra batteries Essential
Backpack/duffel bag (70-90L) Essential
Day pack (25-35L) Essential
Day pack cover (waterproof)
Stuff sacks for backpack contents

Other Items

Sunglasses Recommended
Poncho Optional
2 Water bottles (1L each) Recommended
Water bladder (Camelbak type) Optional
Towel (microfibre) Optional
Pee bottle for nighttime Optional

Personal Items

Toiletries

Lip Balm

First Aid Kit (personal)
Hand Sanitizer

Snacks (high calorie) Optional

Paperwork

Trip Receipt
Passport Essential
Visa Essential
Immunisation Papers Essential
Insurance Documents Essential
Mobile telephone

Weight Limit

You are limited to 15kg of personal gear (excluding day pack). This will be carried by a Porter. Extra Porters can be hired if needed.

Pro Tip

Take your Hiking Boots on the Plane! Just in case your luggage is delayed or lost. Items of clothing can be replaced, but it's hard to replace your hiking boots.

Need Help with Packing?

If you have any questions about what is required, please ask for assistance. Your guide will do a complete gear check before departure.
