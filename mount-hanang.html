MOUNT HANANG

Fourth Highest Mountain in Tanzania • 3,422m

Mount Hanang is the fourth highest mountain in Tanzania, standing at about 3,422 meters (11,524 feet) above sea level. It is located in the central part of Tanzania, specifically in the Hanang District of the Manyara Region. The mountain is part of the East African Rift and features a variety of ecosystems, including montane forests and grasslands.

Location Details

Hanang District, Manyara Region, Central Tanzania. Part of the East African Rift system.

2 Days Ascent Base Camp Available East African Rift

The ascent typically takes two days, with a recommended base camp located near the mountain's summit. Visitors often enjoy the breathtaking views from the top, which can include sights of other nearby peaks and lakes. The area is less crowded than more famous mountains like Kilimanjaro, making it an attractive option for those seeking a more serene hiking experience.
