KILI TREKS

Mount Kilimanjaro Trekking Routes & Prices

Climbing Mount Kilimanjaro (5,895m) is a once-in-a-lifetime adventure. There are 7 main trekking routes, each offering a different experience in terms of scenery, difficulty, success rate, and comfort. Choosing the right route is key to a successful summit.

Kilimanjaro Routes Overview

There are 7 official routes: Lemosho, Machame, Marangu, Rongai, Umbwe, Shira, and Northern Circuit

1. Lemosho Route

$2,200 - $3,200
Moderate Success: 90%+ Camping 7-9 days

Best Overall - Most scenic route, excellent acclimatization, high summit success rate

Highlights: Rainforest, Shira Plateau, panoramic views

Best for: First-time climbers who want high success and amazing views

2. Machame Route

$1,800 - $2,800
Moderate-Challenging Success: 80-85% Camping 6-7 days

Most Popular - Very scenic, good acclimatization, well-tested route

Highlights: "Climb high, sleep low" acclimatization

Best for: Adventurous trekkers

3. Marangu Route

$1,400 - $2,000
Moderate Success: 65-75% Huts 5-6 days

Comfort Route - Budget-friendly, comfortable hut accommodation, shortest route

Less acclimatization = lower success

Best for: Budget travelers & beginners

4. Rongai Route

$1,900 - $2,700
Moderate Success: 80-85% Camping 6-7 days

Quiet Route - Only route from the north (near Kenya), less crowded, drier climate

Best for: Those seeking a quiet experience

5. Northern Circuit

$2,800 - $3,800
Moderate Success: 90%+ Camping 8-10 days

Best Success Rate - Longest route → best acclimatization, least crowded, 360° views

Best for: High success & premium experience

6. Umbwe Route

$1,700 - $2,500
Very Difficult Success: ~60% Camping 5-6 days

Most Challenging - Fast and direct route, very scenic but steep

Poor acclimatization → not recommended for beginners

Best for: Experienced climbers only

7. Shira Route

$2,000 - $2,800
Moderate Success: ~85% Camping 6-7 days

Similar to Lemosho - Starts at higher altitude

Not ideal for beginners due to altitude start
