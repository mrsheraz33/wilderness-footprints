SOUTHERN SAFARI

Discover Tanzania's Pristine Wilderness

Southern Tanzania offers some of the country's most pristine and less-traveled national parks, making it an excellent destination for a safari experience. Here's an overview of key parks and features in the southern safari circuit:

1. Ruaha National Park

Features: Ruaha is the largest national park in Tanzania and is known for its stunning landscapes, including rolling savannahs, riverine forests, and rugged mountains.

Wildlife: It is home to a significant population of elephants, lions, and various antelope species. The park is also known for its diverse birdlife, with over 500 species recorded.

Activities: Game drives, walking safaris, and birdwatching are popular activities. The park offers a more remote and exclusive safari experience compared to northern parks.

2. Selous Game Reserve / Nyerere National Park

Features: Selous is one of the largest game reserves in Africa and is a UNESCO World Heritage site. It has a variety of ecosystems, including bushland, wetlands, and rivers.

Wildlife: It boasts a rich diversity of wildlife, including elephants, black rhinos, wildebeests, and a large population of hippos and crocodiles in the Rufiji River.

Activities: The reserve allows for walking and boat safaris in addition to traditional game drives, offering a unique perspective on the wildlife and landscapes.

3. Mikumi National Park

Features: Located near the town of Mikumi, this park is easily accessible and known for its flat savannahs and a backdrop of the Udzungwa Mountains.

Wildlife: Common sightings include elephants, zebras, giraffes, and a variety of bird species. It provides a great experience for those looking for a quick safari.

Activities: Game drives and guided walks are available, making it suitable for both short visits and longer stays.

4. Udzungwa Mountains National Park

Features: Known for its stunning mountain landscapes and rich biodiversity, this park is a part of the Eastern Arc Mountains.

Wildlife: It is famous for its endemic species, including several types of monkeys and unique flora. Hiking trails lead to beautiful waterfalls and scenic vistas.

Activities: Trekking and hiking are the main activities, attracting nature enthusiasts and those interested in botany and conservation.

General Tips for Southern Safaris

Best Time to Visit: The dry season (June to October) is the best time for wildlife viewing as animals gather around water sources.

Accommodation: Options range from luxury lodges and tented camps to budget accommodations. Many lodges offer guided safari experiences.

Getting There: Accessing these parks typically involves a combination of domestic flights and road transfers, with many itineraries suggesting starting in Dar es Salaam.

Southern Tanzania is ideal for those seeking an authentic and less commercialized safari experience, allowing visitors to enjoy Tanzania's natural beauty and rich wildlife in a tranquil setting.
